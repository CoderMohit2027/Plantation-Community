What We Do


Saytrees plants saplings and supports them to become trees. At SayTrees we have been creating urban and rural forests , support farmers with fruit trees, rejuvenate lakes and water bodies and install solar lamps in villages. SayTrees does all this with support from individuals and corporates which wish to bring change to the society under their CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility) initiatives. SayTrees is an NGO in India registered under section 12A and 80G of Income Tax. SayTrees also holds the FCRA certificate to receive donation from outside India.Planting trees is essential for maintaining ecological balance and enhancing the environment. Trees absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen, helping to combat climate change and improve air quality. They provide habitats for wildlife, support biodiversity, and stabilize soil, preventing erosion. Trees also play a critical role in regulating temperatures, reducing the urban heat island effect, and maintaining the water cycle, which helps prevent floods and maintain groundwater levels. Additionally, trees enhance the beauty of landscapes, offer recreational spaces, and can boost property values and reduce energy costs by providing shade. Overall, planting trees is a vital action for sustaining a healthy and vibrant ecosystem.o protect trees, ensure they receive adequate water and nutrients through regular watering and mulching, which helps retain soil moisture and suppress weeds. Pruning dead or diseased branches promotes healthy growth and prevents the spread of disease. Use protective barriers to shield trees from physical damage caused by lawn equipment or construction activities. Combat pests and diseases with appropriate treatments and monitor for any signs of infestation. Educating the community about the value of trees and involving them in conservation efforts fosters a collective sense of responsibility. Additionally, supporting and enforcing policies that prevent deforestation and encourage reforestation are crucial for long-term tree preservation.

How To Protect


Protecting trees involves several strategies to ensure their health and longevity. Regular watering and mulching help maintain soil moisture and prevent weed growth, while pruning removes dead or diseased branches, promoting healthy growth. Guarding against pests and diseases through appropriate treatments is also crucial. Trees should be protected from physical damage caused by lawn equipment or construction activities, which can be achieved by installing protective barriers or fencing. Educating communities about the importance of trees and encouraging local participation in conservation efforts further supports tree protection.
